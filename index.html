ABOUT BMI

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a measure used to assess a person's body weight in relation to their height. It provides a numerical indicator of whether an individual is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. However, BMI alone may not accurately reflect an individual's overall health as it does not account for factors like muscle mass or distribution of fat.

Factors affecting BMI include:

  • Age
  • Sex
  • Ethinicity
  • Muscle Mass
  • Developmental Factors
  • Genetic Factors
  • Physical Activity
  • Diet
  • Environemntal Factors
  • Social Factors

Below is an explanation about BMI Levels:

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5 indicates being underweight, which may suggest insufficient body weight relative to height, potentially indicating malnutrition or other health concerns.
  • Normal Weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered normal, indicating a healthy weight range relative to height, associated with lower risk of health problems.
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9 suggests being overweight, indicating excess body weight relative to height, which may increase the risk of various health issues like heart disease and diabetes.
    • Obese: BMI of 30 or higher indicates obesity, characterized by excessive body fat accumulation, significantly raising the risk of serious health conditions such as heart disease, stroke, and certain cancers.
